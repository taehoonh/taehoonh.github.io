My interest is in developing and applying novel statistical methods to better design biological and clinical studies. Currently, I am working as a biostatistican at the Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory. I collaborate extensively with a focus on cancer related laboratory and clinical studies.

Previously, I worked as a research assistant at Weill Cornell Medicine in the Department of Population Health Sciences, where I worked primarily in breast cancer, colorectal cancer, obesity, and inflammation.

I graduated from Weill Cornell Medicine with a MS in biostatistics and data science in 2019.

Beyond the lab, I am also analyzing brain and liver transcriptome data to identify differentially expressed genes associated with the exposure to ultra-fine dust, PM2.5, under the supervision of Bongsoo Park, PhD at National Insitute of Health and Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Heatlh.
